01 — Why not

Six forces that tilted the world toward patriarchy

Anthropology points to structural pressures — not destiny. Drag the slider to watch power concentrate at a peak, then redistribute around a center.

  1. 1

    Subsistence shift

    Plough agriculture & pastoralism concentrated inheritable wealth in men — "the cow is the enemy of matriliny."

  2. 2

    Colonialism

    Empires overwrote matrilineal succession with patriarchal law and property regimes.

  3. 3

    The matrilineal puzzle

    Men were torn between providing for their sisters' children and their wives' children.

  4. 4

    Warfare

    Patrilocal, patrilineal bands coordinated defense and offense more readily under male lineages.

  5. 5

    State formation

    Patrilineal descent concentrates power vertically — the engine of long-lasting empires.

  6. 6

    World religions

    Sacred texts across major faiths encoded bias against women holding authority.

A critical distinction: scholars like Sanday and Göttner-Abendroth define matriarchy not as women dominating men, but as egalitarian, consensus-based, gift-economy societies. No society that fully excludes men from authority has ever been documented — the goal is balance, not inversion.
