Morning Mist

Category: Nature

This piece began as a single wash of cerulean blue laid onto soaking-wet paper. The pigment was encouraged to pool and run, creating the soft gradients that suggest a landscape dissolving into fog. Fine ink lines were added last — a horizon, a treeline, a lone figure — anchoring the wash before it could escape entirely.

The story of Morning Mist is the story of every watercolorist's first lesson: the paper is in charge. You guide, but the water decides. The result is a collaboration between intention and physics, between the hand that holds the brush and the surface that receives it.

Created with wet-on-wet technique, cerulean blue and burnt sienna washes, fine ink outlines on 300gsm cold-press paper.
