How it works

Six phases. Like a skilled team in a room.

The panel works through a structured flow that mirrors how a strong human team actually gets something done — from agreeing on the question to shipping only finished work.

01
1

Clarify

The panel agrees on what's actually being asked — surfacing the real goal before anyone starts building.

02
2

Explore

Options and ideas go on the table. The panel widens the space of what's possible before narrowing it.

03
3

Narrow

The panel converges on a direction — debating trade-offs and committing to a plan worth executing.

04
4

Execute

The agents draft the deliverables, run code, browse the web, and save real files to ground and verify their work.

05
5

Debrief

The panel reviews what was produced — checking quality, gaps, and whether the work actually meets the brief.

06
6

Stop

A built-in quality check inspects the saved files. If any deliverable is missing, the panel goes back for another round — it won't ship incomplete work.

Features

Built to do what a single chat can't.

Every capability exists for one reason: to turn a prompt into work you can actually trust and use.

Why it's different

A team of minds, not one answer.

A single chatbot gives you one reply and calls it done. Panel gives you a team that debates, verifies, and refuses to ship until the work is actually finished.

A single chatbot prompt

  • One assistant giving one answer
  • A single reply, no structured process
  • Describes work instead of running it
  • Text output only — no real files
  • "Good enough" with no completion check
  • No scored summary of how it went

The Panel system

  • A team of specialized minds with distinct roles
  • Internal debate through structured phases
  • Actually runs and verifies its own work
  • Real saved files — including screenshots
  • Enforced completion that loops back, not "good enough"
  • A scored summary of the whole run
What you get

A complete, auditable work product.

Every run produces a timestamped folder. Not a chat log — a full bundle you can open, audit, and hand off.

  • The deliverable files

    The actual output — a generated website with screenshots, a game, a brief — saved as real files you can open.

  • The full transcript

    Every turn from every agent — the complete conversation showing how the panel reasoned its way to the result.

  • A final summary with a scored rubric

    A clear write-up of how the run went, scored against a rubric so you can judge quality at a glance.

  • Per-round timing data

    How long each round took — useful for benchmarking runs and understanding where the panel spends its effort.
